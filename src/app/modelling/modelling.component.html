Security Breaches​

  • 49% of companies in the USA have suffered at least one data breach according to the International Data Corporation (IDC)​
  • 26% of companies in the USA have suffered a data breach in the past 12 months​
  • Every 39 seconds, a hacking attack is executed which amounts to 2244 attacks per day (University of Maryland)​
  • Estimated that by 2021, an organization will be the target of a ransomware attack every 11 seconds (Herjavec Group)​
  • Although data breaches happen every day, it takes 206 days on average to detect a data breach

New Strategies​

  • Educate and train all staff on the basics of cybersecurity​
  • Limit access to valuable data better by making sure that only authorized users have access to sensitive data​
  • Regular security posture assessments - We should know what our cyber risks are​
  • Construct a cyber breach response plan - We should know what to do if a breach occurs​
  • IT investment into cybersecurity initiatives should be given priority.​
  • 3rd party applications pose security risks and should be properly vetted before being implemented​

Data Breach Costs​​

  • US$3.86 million is the average cost of a data breach (IBM)​
    • Detection and escalation US$1.22 million - (31.1%)​
    • Notification US$0.21 million - (5.4%)​
    • Post breach response US$1.07 million - (27.3%)​
    • Lost business US$1.42 million - (36.2%)​
  • On average, the cost of a stolen record is US$150 (IBM)​
  • A breach comprising of 1 million records carries an average cost of US$42 million (IBM).​

Intelligence based Security​

  • Having an incident response team can reduce the cost of a breach by US$19 per record​
  • By using extensive encryption to protect data, organizations can save US$360 000 on a data breach​
  • Backup solutions such as business continuity plans can save a company US$280 000 on a data breach​
  • Implementing the security analytics tools can save organizations up to US$200 000 on a data breach​
  • Implementing two factor authentication makes it difficult for criminals to gain access, even if a phishing attempt was successful​
